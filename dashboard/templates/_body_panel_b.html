  • Energy stocks headline the weekly build(+)/draw(−) in the native unit (nat-gas as injection/withdrawal); Δ vs same week last year is shown alongside.
  • Flow proxies (crude field production, products supplied) are per-day rates, NOT inventory — they never get a build/draw or a tight/loose verdict; only a labelled week-over-week rate change.
  • The own-history percentile is each series vs its own same-period stored history — not seasonally adjusted, NOT the EIA 5-yr band; below the accrual minimum it shows — (accruing M/N) with no verdict.
  • Grain coverage is thin: 3 annual production + 3 quarterly stocks only. WASDE balance sheet and weekly Crop Progress/Condition are deferred — there is no stocks-to-use here and grain fundamentals are NOT fully covered.
  • Staleness is cadence-bucketed: weekly EIA is release-aware (petroleum Wed / nat-gas Thu, +1 day after a Monday holiday); quarterly/annual USDA are "stale by design" between reports and are not flagged red.
  • Cushing only carries a "% of operable shell capacity" and a near-tank-bottom badge — it is the WTI/CL physical delivery point, the one tank where the absolute level signals a squeeze/backwardation setup. Operable shell (~76M bbl) and the tank-bottom region (~20M bbl) are last-known config constants (EIA publishes them separately, semi-annually); no other tank gets this. With no configured capacity the figure is simply omitted.
  • Inventory tension is fundamentals context (tight → upside tail/move risk → vol bid). The decision lives in Panel D (volatility).
